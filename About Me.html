I am an Associate Research Scientist in Dr. Daniel Salzman's lab at Columbia University. My research has been supported by an NIMH T32 training grant, the Helen Hay Whitney Foundation, and now the NIGMS MOSAIC K99/R00. My research focuses on how dynamic social constructs drive motivated behavior in mice.

I received my PhD in Neurobiology with Dr. Fan Wang at Duke University examining the neural circuitry underlying heightened facial pain perception and neural circuitry underlying satiety. This research was supported by NIDCR's F31 fellowship.

I received my B.A in Biology/Neuroscience and Psychology through the Macaulay Honors College at CUNY Queens College. Under the guidance of Dr. Carolyn Pytte, my undergrauate research focus was understanding the effects of songbird vocalization on neurogenesis in the adult male Zebra finch.
