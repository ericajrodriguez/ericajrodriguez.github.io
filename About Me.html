Currently, I am an Associate Research Scientist in Dr. Daniel Salzman's lab at Columbia University. My research, supported by NIMH's T32 training grant, the Helen Hay Whitney Foundation, & the NIGMS MOSAIC K99/R00, focuses on understanding how dynamic social constructs drive motivated behavior in mice.

I received my PhD in Neurobiology with Dr. Fan Wang at Duke University. My research focus was on dissecting the neural circuitry underlying heightened facial pain perception and the neural micro-circuitry underlying satiety. This research was supported by a NIDCR F31 Diversity Fellowship.

I received my B.A in Biology/Neuroscience & Psychology through the Macaulay Honors College at CUNY Queens College. Under the guidance of Dr. Carolyn Pytte, and with support from NIH's T34 MARC USTAR fellowship, I examined the effects of songbird vocalization on neurogenesis.
